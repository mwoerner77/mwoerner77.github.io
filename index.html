Course Description

Knowledge management theory has emerged as an important field for practice and research in software management. We will study the theories as foundations to provide a rationale for managing knowledge. Students who enrolled in this class should have have already known the definition of knowledge as a resource. This class helps the students use the theoretical reasoning for evaluating the knowledge management process. By the end of this course, the students will master the knowledge management process.

Syllabus

  • Introduction To Knowledge Management
  • History of Knowledge Management
  • Research and Dimensions of Knowledge Management
  • Strategies and Motivations of Knowledge Management
  • Knowledge Management Cycle
  • Knowledge Management Models
  • Knowledge Capture and Codification
  • Knowledge Sharing and Communities of Practice
  • Knowledge Use and Applications
